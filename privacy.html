This Privacy Notice for LOREG ("we," "us," or "our") describes how and why we might access, collect, store, use, and/or share ("process") your personal information when you use our services ("Services"), including when you download and use our mobile application, Skrolla — a free Android app for book discovery. Users scroll through book covers to get personalized recommendations, search a catalog of books, save books to reading lists, and optionally chat with an AI assistant (StoryMode) about a book before reading it. The app uses account/session data to personalize recommendations and is supported by in-app advertising (Google AdMob).

If you do not agree with our policies and practices, please do not use our Services. If you have any questions or concerns, contact us at helloskrolla@gmail.com.

1. What information do we collect?

In short: we collect personal information that you voluntarily provide to us, plus limited device/usage data collected automatically.

Personal information you disclose to us

We collect personal information that you voluntarily provide when you register on the Services, express interest in our products, participate in activities on the Services, or otherwise contact us. This may include:

  • Names
  • Email addresses
  • Usernames

We do not process sensitive information (e.g. racial or ethnic origin, sexual orientation, religious beliefs).

Application data. If you use the app, we may automatically collect device information such as your mobile device ID, model and manufacturer, operating system and version, device/application identification numbers, browser type, hardware model, internet service provider/mobile carrier, and IP address. This is primarily needed to maintain the security and operation of the app, for troubleshooting, and for internal analytics and reporting.

Information automatically collected

We automatically collect certain information when you use the Services — device and usage information, IP address, browser/device characteristics, operating system, language preferences, referring URLs, device name, country, location, and how and when you use our Services. This does not reveal your specific identity on its own.

Log and usage data. Service-related, diagnostic, usage, and performance information our servers automatically collect and record in log files — IP address, device information, browser type, activity in the Services (timestamps, pages/files viewed, searches, features used), and device event information (crash reports, hardware settings).

Google API. Our use of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

In short: depending on where you live (e.g. the EEA, UK, Switzerland, or Canada), you may have rights to access, correct, restrict, or port your data, and to object to processing.

These rights may include the right to (i) request access to and a copy of your personal information, (ii) request rectification or erasure, (iii) restrict processing, (iv) data portability, and (v) not be subject to solely automated decision-making that produces legal or similarly significant effects. If you're in the UK and unhappy with how we've handled your information, you can complain to us directly first; we'll acknowledge within 30 days, investigate without undue delay, and keep you informed. If unresolved, you can refer your complaint to the Information Commissioner's Office (helpline 0303 123 1113). EEA/UK residents may also complain to their Member State/UK data protection authority, and Switzerland residents may contact the Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner.

Withdrawing consent. Where we rely on your consent, you may withdraw it at any time by contacting us — this won't affect the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.

Account information. You can review or change your account information in-app, or by contacting us. Upon a termination request we'll deactivate/delete your account and information from our active databases, though we may retain some data to prevent fraud, troubleshoot, assist investigations, or comply with legal requirements.

Cookies. Most browsers accept cookies by default; you can set your browser to remove or reject them, though this may affect certain features.

No uniform technology standard for recognizing and implementing Do-Not-Track ("DNT") signals has been finalized. We do not currently respond to DNT browser signals or similar mechanisms.
